Eggs: Scrambled or Fried?

There is a lot of discussion around the best way to serve an egg for breakfast. Let's take a look of the pros and cons of two common forms that result in many heated debates.

Scrambling Eggs

The most common form of egg found in most breakfast-food places. It's hard to mess up a scrambled egg, unless you set the heat on max. Pros: -Easy to make, doesn't require much skill -Can turn into an omelet with some seasoning -Can make crispy or fluffy -Great with ketchup Cons:
-Same texture throughout, so it lacks anything special
-Can only mainly be eaten with breakfast food, like sausage or bacon
-Takes physical effort to scramble How to Make Scrambled Eggs
