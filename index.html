Eggs: Scrambled or Fried?

There is a lot of discussion around the best way to serve an egg for breakfast. Let's take a look of the pros and cons of two common forms that result in many heated debates.

Scrambling Eggs

The most common form of egg found in most breakfast-food places. It's hard to mess up a scrambled egg, unless you set the heat on max.
Pros:
-Easy to make, doesn't require much skill
-Can turn into an omelet with some seasoning
-Can make crispy or fluffy
-Great with ketchup
Cons:
-Same texture throughout, so it lacks anything special
-Can only mainly be eaten with breakfast food, like sausage or bacon
-Takes physical effort to scramble

Frying Eggs

This egg form is a little fancier, not often advertised in most breakfast-food places. There's definitely a higher chance of messing up with this egg.
Pros:
-Lots of variation: runny, soft, solid
-Different texture with the yolk and white
-No physical labor required
-Goes with a lot more food, like rice or bread
Cons
-You need to be good at telepathically knowing when the egg is cooked enough
-Can only season the outside of this egg
